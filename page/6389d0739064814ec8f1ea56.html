Job Description

We create ambitious games in a quietly awesome culture. That simple statement is at the heart of what we do here at Possibility Space. Founded by a diverse and experienced team of game and media industry veterans, we are built from the ground up as a distributed studio, allowing developers to live and work where they are happiest. We strive to create an environment where we can do our best work regardless of what we look like, who we love, or how we identify. With support operations in New Orleans, Louisiana, the studio also works to identify, mentor, train, and hire individuals from underserved and overlooked communities and backgrounds. Possibility Space is seeking a UX Systems Engineer to work alongside our design, art and engineering departments to build a great user experience for our new game. You will work across our talented and supportive team to build out a UX design system and UI/UX component library that will make it quick and easy for Possibility Space developers to build well-functioning interfaces easily, and comprehensively address player needs like supporting different control schemes, providing accessibility functionality, and robust localization. If you are passionate about embedding within teams with diverse needs and creating highly usable tools that allow talented game devs to quickly work through their pipelines and produce features and UI that delights players, then this might be an especially exciting opportunity. Reporting to the Systems Engineering Director, this role collaborates with disciplines across the entire project through all stages of development. Core Responsibilities & Objectives Work as part of cross-functional teams to support the design and implementation of UX systems and interface features. Enable new capabilities by writing high-performance, scalable, and reusable code across both the development pipeline and game runtime. Profile and optimize systems to remove bottlenecks and improve performance. Help establish development standards and best practices for UI/UX engineering across the entire project. Core Traits (Skills, Knowledge, Experience) Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science or a related field (or equivalent experience). Proficient with UI development in a commercial game engine or desktop tooling pipeline. Solid analytical problem-solving and debugging skills. Able to write readable, maintainable code with an eye toward debuggability and extensibility. Empathetic and supportive collaboration across many disciplines. Excellent spoken and written communication. Bonus Traits Proficient with C#. Experience developing with Unity. Experience with UI Toolkit. Experience developing for a live, service-based game. Physical Requirements Close vision to monitors and screens, consistently working on computer equipment such as keyboards and monitors, long periods of sitting.
