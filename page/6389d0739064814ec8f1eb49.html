Job Description

Created by Monsters. Built for gamers.™ Truly Likeable Monsters (TLM), is a world-class video game company focused on development for some of the biggest games in the industry. Our mission is to break down barriers in the video game industry and make game tech available to all game developers regardless of size. We are looking for good humans with incredible talent to make great games. Position Summary: Assistant Producer/Development Manager We are looking for a development manager who is passionate about both process and gaming, to provide daily production leadership to the project and its teams. 100% Remote position & other cool benefits Working with an international team on AA or AAA games in an Unreal Studio You will report to a project Development Director on one of our games. You will help the leadership group manage project and resource planning, balance scope with capacity, track progress, and manage risks and mitigation. You are growing your skills in the area of content pipelines and feature development. You understand the iterative nature of creative processes and how to empower them. You will help build healthy, high functioning relationships with external vendors. You are a force multiplier for the team. Responsibilities Establish direction & strategy for a team within a project while ensuring alignment with internal & external leadership. Monitor & adjust capacity plans for your team to address skill gaps and identify funding needs. Be the point of escalation for conflict management and problem solving for your team. Partner with DDs at all levels to help manage the priorities and deadlines for a project. Help establish and maintain team health standards. Identify and implement process improvement initiatives across your team. Approve plans, schedules, and status reports within your group. Anticipate and solve project dependencies between internal and external groups. Qualifications You are passionate about managing creative teams. You have 2+ years experience working as a project manager in AAA game development. You have experience managing internal and external teams; you have many conflict resolution tools in your tool belt. You are comfortable talking across all levels of an organization; you always know the status of your team and can visualize that status for any audience. You understand the complexities and requirements of game production and how to manage them.
