About Us Twitch is the world’s biggest live streaming service, with global communities built around gaming, entertainment, music, sports, cooking, and more. It’s where millions of people come together to chat, interact, and make their own entertainment. We’re about community, inside and out. You’ll find coworkers who are eager to team up, collaborate, and smash (or elegantly solve) problems together. We’re on a quest to empower live communities, so if this sounds good to you, see what we’re up to on LinkedIn and Twitter, get interviewing tips on Instagram, and discover projects we’re solving on our Blog. About the Role Amazon Interactive Video Service (IVS) is a managed live video service, within Amazon Web Services (AWS), built on top of Twitch Video. As one of the largest live video networks, IVS powers companies using live video to uniquely engage and build communities, just as it does for Twitch, where a billion+ hours are live streamed monthly. The IVS Data Platform team develops and operates the platform that enables IVS's data-informed decision-making. Our data pipeline receives data in real-time and we forward live data to numerous systems while enabling IVS data engineers to extract the datasets they need to empower our business analysts. We provide tools to ingest, store, transform, move, and understand data. As a software engineer on IVS Data Platform, you will deliver the next generation of IVS data technology. Using Go and Python, you will build tools which are reliable and scale. You'll review and shape the work of your peers and participate in a high performing engineering team. You can work in San Francisco, CA, Seattle, WA, Austin, TX, or remotely in the USA or Canada. You Will: Develop new capabilities in our data warehouses and pipelines. Improve the reliability, flexibility, and scalability of our existing tools. Collaborate on our vision as we scale. You Have: 5+ years with software engineering experience. 2+ years of experience in Go or Python. 2+ years working with distributed, highly available systems. Minimum of bachelor's degree in Computer Science or prior relevant engineering experiences. Experience with data structures and algorithms. Proficient experience in one of the following languages: Go, Python, Ruby, Java, and C++ Bonus Points You have experience with AWS data services such as Amazon Redshift, Amazon S3, Amazon Kinesis, and AWS Glue. You have made petabytes of data useful to your customers.
